Dell Solution Brief – Style Guide

1. Executive Summary

This style guide documents the proven design and content philosophy found in the Dell solution brief. It emphasizes clarity, trustworthiness, and a modern technological look, using clean layouts, Dell Blue highlights, and a confident but neutral tone. Consistency, legibility, and simplicity are always prioritized.

2. Visual Identity Standards

Color Palette

  • Primary: Dell Blue #0076CE
  • Secondary: Soft Grey #F4F4F4, Steel Grey #222222
  • Accent: Safety Red #D4002B, Success Green #19A871
  • Background: White #FFFFFF
Do: Use Dell Blue for headers, highlights, and lines.
Don't: Use multicolored backgrounds or excessive accent colors.

Logo Usage & Placement

  • Place the Dell logo at the top left or bottom right on the first page.
  • Minimum clear space: At least height of "D" from wordmark on all sides.
  • Logo size: ~35-45px print, ~90-120px digital screen width.
  • Never distort or recolor logo.

Image Style & Treatment

  • Use professional imagery supporting the document narrative, such as technology, workflow, or data visuals.
  • Images should have rounded corners (5px radius) and subtle drop shadows (#E0E0E0, 6px, 40% opacity).
  • Avoid clip art, low-quality stock, or non-thematic decoration.

Icons & Graphics

  • Prefer clean, line-based icons in Dell Blue.
  • Icon sizes: 32px–56px.
  • Icons should be geometric, unified in style, and sans-serif.

4. Layout Templates

Page Structure & Grids

  • Content is left-aligned, with a 2–3 column grid for multisecton pages.
  • Standard margins: 40px outer, 24px between columns.
  • Sections separated by horizontal lines (#0076CE, 2–4px thickness).

Headers & Footers

  • Header: Document title left, logo right.
  • Footer: Copyright, trademarks, brief CTA info (8pt, muted grey).

Section Organization & Content Hierarchy

  • Bold H2 headers for major sections, H3 for subsections. Start with a short intro summary.
  • Bullet lists with at least 12px spacing between items.
  • CTAs in blue boxes with white text.

5. Content Standards

Tone & Messaging

  • Tone: Confident, professional, accessible.
  • Messaging: Guidance, impact, trusted expertise.

Writing Style

  • Active voice, concise sentences.
  • Structure: Intro → Challenge → Solution → CTA.
  • Use "your organization" for direct engagement.
  • Format: Bold for emphasis; italics for product/service names.
  • Avoid jargon unless explained.

Calls-to-Action & Data Presentation

  • CTAs: Imperative sentences ("Get started with the Dell AI Factory", "Join the conversation").
  • Data: Present facts with short statements; bullet lists for multipoint info.
  • Cite sources succinctly (e.g., "Based on Dell analysis, July 2024").
Do: Outline next steps with clear, positive verbs.
Don't: Fill with technical detail or lengthy paragraphs.

7. Quick Reference Guide

  • Dell Blue (Primary): Headings, highlights (#0076CE)
  • Font: Segoe UI/Arial for all content; bold for titles
  • Grid: 2–3 columns, 40px margin, 24px columns
  • Logo: Top left/bottom right, undistorted, not cropped
  • Imagery: Professional, rounded corners, subtle shadow
  • Tone: Confident, concise, helpful
  • CTA: Blue box, short imperative
  • Section headings: Bold, #0076CE, clear/direct
Do: Start with executive summary, keep sections tight, use hierarchy.
Don't: Mix fonts, colors, or icon styles.
